# Price Volume Mix (PVM) Analysis

## What is PVM?

PVM = Price + Volume + Mix

Purpose:

PVM helps analysts understand WHY revenue changed.

Instead of asking:

Revenue changed?

We ask:

Revenue changed because of:

1. Price change?
2. Quantity change?
3. Product/customer mix change?

---

## PVM Components

Revenue Change

↓

Price Effect

Volume Effect

Mix Effect

↓

Final Variance Explanation

---

## Price Effect

Formula:

Price Effect =
(Actual Price - Budget Price)
× Actual Quantity

Meaning:

Revenue impact caused only because price changed.

Example:

Budget:

Qty = 100

Price = 10

Revenue = 1000

Actual:

Qty = 100

Price = 12

Revenue = 1200

Price Effect:

(12 - 10)
×100

= +200

Interpretation:

Revenue increased because price increased.

Business Questions:

- Were discounts given?
- Was premium pricing introduced?
- Did competitors force price reduction?
- Did product positioning change?

Possible Root Causes:

- Discount campaign
- Seasonal promotion
- Competitor pressure
- Pricing strategy update

---

## Volume Effect

Formula:

Volume Effect =
(Actual Qty - Budget Qty)
× Budget Price

Meaning:

Revenue impact caused because quantity changed.

Example:

Budget:

Qty = 100

Price = 10

Revenue = 1000

Actual:

Qty = 120

Price = 10

Revenue = 1200

Volume Effect:

(120 - 100)
×10

= +200

Interpretation:

Revenue increased because more units were sold.

Business Questions:

- Did demand increase?
- Customer growth?
- Seasonality?
- Inventory availability?
- Marketing campaign impact?

Possible Root Causes:

- Increased demand
- Better marketing
- Product launch
- Customer acquisition
- Festival season

---

## Mix Effect

Meaning:

Revenue/profit impact caused because product composition changed.

Example:

Products:

Basic

Margin = 20%

Enterprise

Margin = 60%

Budget Mix:

Basic = 20%

Enterprise = 80%

Actual Mix:

Basic = 80%

Enterprise = 20%

Revenue:

May remain stable.

Profit:

Falls.

Reason:

High-margin products reduced.

Business Questions:

- Did customers shift products?
- Did premium customers leave?
- Did low margin products grow?
- Did region mix change?

Possible Root Causes:

- Enterprise churn
- Discount-heavy sales
- Product strategy change
- Customer segment shift

---

## Full Example

Budget:

Qty = 100

Price = 10

Revenue = 1000

Actual:

Qty = 120

Price = 8

Revenue = 960

Price Effect:

(8 - 10)
×120

= -240

Volume Effect:

(120 - 100)
×10

= +200

Revenue Change:

960 - 1000

= -40

Interpretation:

Price reduction increased demand.

However:

Revenue still decreased because price impact exceeded quantity gain.

Possible Story:

Company gave discounts.

Customers purchased more.

Discount effect was larger than volume improvement.

Final Result:

Revenue down.
