# P&L Basics

## What is a P&L Statement?

P&L (Profit and Loss Statement) shows how a company earns money and where it spends money.

Flow:

Revenue- COGS
= Gross Profit

Gross Profit- Operating Expenses
= EBITDA

EBITDA- Interest- Taxes- Depreciation- Amortization
= Net Profit

---

## Revenue

Meaning:

Money earned from selling products or services.

Formula:

Revenue = Quantity × Selling Price

Questions Analysts Ask:

- Revenue by product?
- Revenue by region?
- Revenue growth?
- Is growth because of price or quantity?
- Which customer segment contributes most?

---

## COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)

Meaning:

Direct costs required to produce or deliver product/service.

Examples:

Manufacturing:
- Raw materials
- Factory labor
- Packaging

SaaS:
- Cloud cost
- Support team
- Licenses

Formula:

Gross Profit = Revenue - COGS

---

## Gross Profit

Formula:

Gross Profit = Revenue - COGS

Meaning:

Money remaining after direct costs.

Example:

Revenue = 100 Cr

COGS = 40 Cr

Gross Profit = 60 Cr

---

## Operating Expenses (OpEx)

Examples:

- Marketing
- Salaries
- Rent
- Travel
- Admin cost

---

## EBITDA

Formula:

EBITDA = Gross Profit - Operating Expenses

Meaning:

Operational profitability before financing and taxes.

Used By:

- CFO
- FP&A teams
- Investors
- PE firms

---

## Net Profit

Formula:

Net Profit =
EBITDA- Interest- Taxes- Depreciation- Amortization

Meaning:

Final profit remaining after all expenses.
