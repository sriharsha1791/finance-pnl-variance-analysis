# KPI Dictionary

| Metric | Formula | Meaning | Business Questions |
|---------|---------|---------|-------------------|
| Revenue | Qty × Price | Sales generated | Revenue by region? Product? Growth? |
| Gross Profit | Revenue - COGS | Profit after direct cost | Which products are profitable? |
| Gross Margin % | Gross Profit / Revenue ×100 | Profitability ratio | Margin improving or falling? |
| EBITDA | Gross Profit - OpEx | Operational profit | Operational efficiency? |
| Net Profit | EBITDA - Interest - Tax - Depreciation - Amortization | Final profit | Overall company performance? |
| Variance % | (Actual - Budget)/Budget ×100 | Gap from target | Which segment missed target? |
| Price Effect | (Actual Price - Budget Price) × Actual Qty | Revenue impact due to price | Did pricing change revenue? |
| Volume Effect | (Actual Qty - Budget Qty) × Budget Price | Revenue impact due to quantity | Did demand change? |
| Mix Effect | Total Variance - Price Effect - Volume Effect | Revenue impact due to product/customer mix | Did customer behavior change? |

## Gross Profit

Purpose:

Shows profit after direct costs.

Questions:

- Which products generate profit?
- Which regions perform better?
- Is COGS increasing?

Risk:

Revenue may increase while gross profit decreases.

---

## Gross Margin %

Meaning:

Measures profitability efficiency.

Example:

Revenue = 100

Gross Profit = 60

Margin = 60%

Risk Signals:

- Margin erosion
- Cost increase
- Product mix change
- Discounting

---

## EBITDA

Purpose:

Users:

- Investors
- PE Firms

Questions:

- Operational improvement?
- Trend analysis?

---

## Net Profit

---

## Variance %

Purpose:

Measures target achievement.

Questions:

- Which segment missed?
- Region issue?
- Price issue?
- Volume issue?

---

## Price Effect

Purpose:

Detect pricing impact.

Possible Reasons:

- Discount campaigns
- Competitor pricing
- Promotions
- Premium pricing

Formula:

(Actual Price - Budget Price)
× Actual Qty

---

## Volume Effect

Purpose:

Detect demand change.

Possible Reasons:

- Customer growth
- Seasonality
- Marketing campaigns
- Product launches

Formula:

(Actual Qty - Budget Qty)
× Budget Price

---

## Mix Effect

Purpose:

Identify product/customer composition changes.

Example:

Enterprise ↓

Basic ↑

Result:

Revenue stable

Profit ↓

Reason:

High margin products reduced.

Concept:

Mix Shift

↓

Margin Erosion
